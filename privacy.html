Privacy policy

How Deli Winthrope Occupational Therapy collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal and health information.

This privacy policy applies to Deli Winthrope Occupational Therapy (ABN 47 219 304 558), operated by Deli Winthrope, a registered occupational therapist (AHPRA OCC0001234567). It covers how we handle personal and health information collected through this website and through the provision of occupational therapy services.

We are bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), and the health records legislation of Victoria. As an AHPRA-registered practitioner, Deli is also bound by the AHPRA Privacy Policy and the relevant professional code of conduct.

Last updated: April 2026

What information we collect

Information you provide directly

When you contact us through the website intake form, by email, or by phone, we may collect:

  • Your name, email address, and phone number
  • Your child's name, age, and relevant health or developmental history
  • Information about your child's NDIS plan, including plan management type and funding details
  • Any other information you choose to share in your initial enquiry

Information collected during therapy

Once a therapeutic relationship begins, we collect and maintain health records that may include:

  • Intake assessment notes and session notes
  • Standardised assessment results (e.g. Sensory Processing Measure, Beery VMI)
  • Correspondence with schools, paediatricians, and other treating professionals (with your consent)
  • Photographs or videos of equipment or activities (only with explicit written consent and only for clinical purposes)
  • Reports and recommendations prepared for NDIS plan reviews, school funding applications, or other purposes

Information collected automatically

This website does not use advertising trackers, third-party analytics, or cookies beyond those required to serve the page. If analytics are used in the future, they will be privacy-first (e.g. Plausible or Fathom) and will not collect personally identifiable information.

How we use your information

We use the information we collect only for the purposes for which it was provided, or purposes directly related to those. Specifically:

  • To respond to your enquiry and determine whether our services are appropriate for your child
  • To provide occupational therapy services and maintain clinical records
  • To communicate with you about your child's progress and sessions
  • To prepare reports, assessments, and NDIS documentation
  • To invoice for services, including through NDIS plan management systems
  • To meet our obligations under AHPRA registration requirements, the Health Records Act 2001 (Vic), and the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Framework

Who we share your information with

We do not share your personal or health information with any third party without your explicit consent, except where required or permitted by law. With your consent, information may be shared with:

  • Schools and teachers: to support your child's learning and wellbeing in the classroom
  • General practitioners and paediatricians: to coordinate care and share assessment findings
  • NDIS plan managers and support coordinators: for invoicing and service coordination purposes
  • Other allied health professionals: where a multidisciplinary approach is in your child's best interest

We may also be required to disclose information without your consent where:

  • There is a risk of serious harm to your child or another person
  • We are required to do so by a court order or other legal obligation
  • Disclosure is required under mandatory reporting obligations

How we store your information

All health records are stored securely using encrypted, password-protected systems that meet or exceed AHPRA requirements for health record management. Physical documents are stored in a locked location. We do not store health information on personal devices or on systems outside Australia without your consent.

Our session note and records system complies with Australian data sovereignty requirements. Your data is stored on servers located in Australia.

Retention period

In line with Victorian health records legislation and AHPRA requirements, we retain health records for:

  • Adults: a minimum of 7 years from the date of the last entry
  • Children: until the person turns 25, or 7 years from the date of the last entry, whichever is later

After the retention period expires, records are securely destroyed or de-identified.

Accessing or correcting your information

You have the right to request access to the personal and health information we hold about you or your child. You also have the right to request that we correct information that is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date.

To make a request, please contact us at deli@winthrope.com.au or by phone on 0400 000 000. We will respond within 30 days. In most cases, access is provided free of charge. If a request is unusually complex, we will advise you of any applicable fee before proceeding.

In some circumstances, access may be declined, for example where providing access would pose a serious threat to your child's safety, or where it would unreasonably impact the privacy of another person. We will explain our reasons in writing if we decline a request.

Complaints

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information, we encourage you to contact us first so we can try to resolve the matter:

  • Email: deli@winthrope.com.au
  • Phone: 0400 000 000

If you are not satisfied with our response, you can lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au, or with the Health Complaints Commissioner (HCC) Victoria at hcc.vic.gov.au.

Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. The current version is always available on this page. If changes are material, we will notify existing clients by email.
