How I can help

Areas of support

I draw on my background as both a paediatric OT and a behaviour support practitioner to offer support that addresses the whole picture, not just one piece of it. The strategies I recommend are neuro-affirming, grounded in evidence and built to fit into real family life.

Whether you're navigating everyday challenges or more complex needs, the goal is always the same: insight, clarity, and a collaborative plan that creates meaningful and lasting change.

Transitions

Moving between activities and settings is one of the most demanding tasks for a dysregulated nervous system. I help families understand what makes transitions hard for their child and build practical strategies that reduce the friction and provide support in these times.

To and from school or daycare Between activities Drop-off and pick-up New school years

Routines

Predictable routines reduce the cognitive and sensory load on children who find daily structure challenging. I work with families to build routines that are realistic, consistent and achievable, leading to a more regulated and connected household.

Morning routines Evening routines Mealtimes Sleep

Dysregulation and meltdowns

I help families understand what is driving dysregulation and outburst behaviours, build their child's regulation capacity over time, and develop responses that help de-escalate and prevent.

Emotion coaching Sensory regulation Outburst behaviours

Boundary setting and toddler battles

Boundaries feel impossible when every "no" triggers dysregulation. I support families to set clear, consistent limits that their child can understand — limits that actually hold and lead to a reduction in power struggles and improved regulation over time.

Boundary setting Toddler battles

Family dynamics

The whole family is affected when one child is struggling. I support families to navigate sibling dynamics, the arrival of a new baby, parental preference, and the everyday load that comes with complex needs.

Sibling challenges New arrivals Parental preference

Social connection

Building and maintaining friendships can be genuinely hard for children who process the world differently. I support children and families to develop the skills and strategies that make social connection feel more possible, in a way that is neuro-affirming and honours who a child is at their core.

Peer relationships Play skills Social communication

School and daycare support

I work directly with teachers and educators to share strategies, align approaches, and make sure the plan at home is supported in the classroom too. I can attend meetings, observe in the classroom, and provide written summaries for school staff.

Educator consultation Engagement in learning Classroom strategies School readiness skills

Community access

Outings, appointments, shopping, and community participation can be overwhelming for children with complex needs. I support families to build the skills and strategies that make community life more manageable.

Community participation Sensory strategies Independence building

Report writing

I prepare clear, thorough reports for NDIS plan reviews, funding applications, and clinical purposes. Reports are written in plain language and tailored to the audience, whether that's a plan manager, a school, or a paediatrician.

NDIS reporting Functional capacity assessments Complex behaviour reporting
Collaboration

Working with the whole team around your child.

Having worked in a transdisciplinary model for a decade, I place a strong emphasis on collaboration. I work closely with the professionals already involved in your child's care, including speech pathologists, psychologists, paediatricians, and support coordinators, to make sure the whole team is aligned and working towards shared goals.
