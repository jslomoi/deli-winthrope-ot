How it works

Accessing OT through your NDIS plan

If your child has an NDIS plan that includes funding for Therapeutic Supports (usually under Capacity Building), you can use those funds to access occupational therapy sessions with me.

You don't need a referral. You don't need to call the NDIS. You book a call with me, we work out whether I'm a good fit, and I'll give you a service agreement to sign before we start. Your plan manager or support coordinator can handle the rest if you have one.

I'll invoice directly against your plan after each session. You'll get an invoice that shows the NDIS line item, the session date, and the amount. If you're plan-managed, your plan manager handles payment. If you're self-managed, you pay me directly and then claim reimbursement from the NDIS portal.

Plan types

Which type of plan do you have?

Plan-managed

A plan manager pays your providers on your behalf. You give me your plan manager's details, I send invoices to them, and they pay me. You don't need to handle any money.

What you need to give me: your plan manager's name and email address.

Self-managed

You manage your plan funds yourself. I invoice you directly after each session; you pay me, then claim reimbursement through the NDIS myplace portal. You have the most flexibility with provider choice.

What you need to give me: nothing extra. I'll send invoices to you directly.

Agency-managed (NDIA-managed)

The NDIA pays providers directly. Because I'm a registered NDIS provider, I can work with agency-managed plans. I claim payment via the NDIS portal after each session using your plan details.

What you need to give me: your NDIS number and plan start/end dates.

Plan reviews

Reports for plan reviews

If your child's plan is coming up for review and you need an occupational therapy report, I can help. Plan review reports typically include:

  • A summary of current functional capacity and how it has changed since the last review
  • Goals for the next plan period, written to align with NDIS goal language
  • Recommended support hours and a rationale for those hours
  • Recommended line items and approximate annual budget
  • Any specialist equipment recommendations (if applicable)

Allow 4–6 weeks before your plan review date to request a report. If you're in a rush, let me know and I'll tell you honestly whether I can help in the timeframe.
